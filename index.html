About Us

We are the robotics team at Vaughn International Studies Academy. Our mentor is Mr. Whitmer. We are a hardworking group of students who are ready to make robots. We are determined to build the best robot possible. When competition is near, we give it our all and don't back down until we are victorious.

Building

The building team takes everyone's ideas and put them together to figure out how we are going to design our robot. The outcome of the robot depends on what the challenge is.The robot should be able to do what the challenges asks of it. They also learn about the parts of the robot and what they do or what they can be used for in competition.

Programming

The programming team program the robot to do different things depending on what the challenge is and depending on what the building team is building. They usually all work together or if not in separate groups, they usually always use robot-c and for practice they use code academy. They are constantly working on the programming of the robot and they are constantly learning new ways of programming.

PSA

The PSA team thinks of ideas on how to help the robotics team. They are constantly thinking of new ideas on how to raise money to help the robotics team. They work on the website contacting people, organizations, and other groups to see if they want to help us raise money to help us get new parts for the robot. When other teams are in need they go and help over, ever group has some basic knowledge on the other groups.

What is FIRST?

FIRST means For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology. It's mission is to inspire young people to be science and technology leaders, by engaging them in exciting mentor-based programs that build science, engineering and technology skills, that inspire innovation, and that foster well-rounded life capabilities including self-confidence, communication, and leadership.

FTC is designed for students in grades 7-12 to compete head to head, using a sports model. Teams are responsible for designing, building, and programming their robots to compete in an alliance format against other teams. The robot kit is reusable from year-to-year and is programmed using a variety of languages. Teams, including coaches, mentors and volunteers, are required to develop strategy and build robots based on sound engineering principles. Awards are given for the competition as well as for community outreach, design, and other real-world accomplishments.
